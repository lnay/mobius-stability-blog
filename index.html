Mobius transforms on Bridgeland stability conditions

Copy of proof (not self contained, to be changed)

This is a copy of the proof from my thesis for the underlying theorem in the above about the autoequivalences of the derived category acting of stability conditions modulo GL2̃(ℝ)+\widetilde{\mathrm{GL}_2}(\mathbb{R})^{+}, via Mobius transforms. It does not contain its context from the containing section in my thesis, I’ll likely revisit this to make it self-contained.

First, note that the action of autoequivalences of the triangulated category on the space of stability conditions commutes with the action of GL2̃(ℝ)+\widetilde{\mathrm{GL}_2}(\mathbb{R})^{+} So the action of cohomological Fourier-Mukai transforms on stability conditions, does descend to an action on stability conditions modulo GL2̃(ℝ)+\widetilde{\mathrm{GL}_2}(\mathbb{R})^{+}. Furthermore, Proposition gives us that the stability conditions given by the parametrisation in Definition can be chosen as representatives

Next, Proposition gives that the stability conditions are determined by their central charge up to even shifts, so modulo GL2̃(ℝ)+\widetilde{\mathrm{GL}_2}(\mathbb{R})^{+}, they are determined uniquely. Therefore, we need only consider the action of ΦH\Phi^H on central charges 𝒵:Hevenalg(𝕋)→ℂ\mathcal{Z}\colon H_{\mathrm{even}}^{\mathrm{alg}}(\mathbb{T}) \to \mathbb{C} modulo GL2̃(ℝ)+\widetilde{\mathrm{GL}_2}(\mathbb{R})^{+} (which reduces to an action of GL2(ℝ)+\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})^{+}). The central charges of the stability conditions σα,β\sigma_{\alpha, \beta} are given by 𝒵β+iα\mathcal{Z}_{\beta + i\alpha} when using the notation defined in the following.

{v∈Hevenalg(𝕋)⊗ℤℂ:Δ(v)=0}ℂ*→homℤ(Hevenalg(𝕋),ℂ)GL2(ℝ)+[v]↦[⟨v,_⟩]\begin{align*} \frac{ \left\{ v \in H_{\mathrm{even}}^{\mathrm{alg}}(\mathbb{T}) \otimes_\mathbb{Z}\mathbb{C} \colon \Delta(v) = 0 \right\} }{\mathbb{C}^{*}} &\to \frac{ \hom_{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{\mathrm{even}}^{\mathrm{alg}}(\mathbb{T}), \mathbb{C}) }{\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})^{+}} \\ \left[ v \right] &\mapsto [ \left< v, \_ \right> ] \end{align*}

Next note that Γ\Gamma from Definition descends to the bijection [Γ]:{v∈Hevenalg(𝕋)⊗ℤℂ:Δ(v)=0}ℂ*→ℂℙ2[exp(zℓ)]↦(1z)[(0,0,1)]↦(01).\begin{align*} [\Gamma] \colon \frac{ \left\{ v \in H_{\mathrm{even}}^{\mathrm{alg}}(\mathbb{T}) \otimes_\mathbb{Z}\mathbb{C} \colon \Delta(v) = 0 \right\} }{\mathbb{C}^{*}} &\to \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2 \\ [\exp(z \ell)] &\mapsto \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ z \end{pmatrix} \\ [(0, 0, 1)] &\mapsto \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} . \end{align*}

These can be composed to give f:ℂℙ2→homℤ(Hevenalg(𝕋),ℂ)GL2(ℝ)+[u]↦[⟨exp(Γ−1(u)ℓ),_⟩]\begin{align*} f \colon \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2 &\to \frac{ \hom_{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{\mathrm{even}}^{\mathrm{alg}}(\mathbb{T}), \mathbb{C}) }{\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})^{+}} \\ \left[ u \right] &\mapsto \left[ \left< \exp( \Gamma^{-1}(u) \ell), \_ \right> \right] \end{align*} so that the equivalence class f([(1β+iα)T])f\left( \left[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & \beta + i\alpha \end{pmatrix}^T \right] \right) contains the central charge for σα,β\sigma_{\alpha, \beta}. Finally, note that ΦH\Phi^H descends to a projective linear map [ΦH]\left[\Phi^H\right] on ℂℙ2\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2 (which are naturally identified with Möbius transformations).

With this notation, we now have a clean way of describing the action of ΦH\Phi^H on the central charges of the stability conditions (modulo GL2(ℝ)+\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})^{+}), which are contained in the image of ff. This action shall be related to the action of [ΦH]\left[\Phi^H\right] on ℂℙ2\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2:

ΦH⋅f([u])=[⟨u,(ΦH)−1(_)⟩]=[⟨ΦH(u),_⟩]=f([ΦH]([u]))\begin{align*} \Phi^H \cdot f([u]) &= \left[ \left< u, (\Phi^H)^{-1}(\_) \right> \right] \\ &= \left[ \left< \Phi^H(u), \_ \right> \right] \\ &= f\left(\left[\Phi^H\right]([u])\right) \end{align*} Now consider the action [ΦH]\left[\Phi^H\right] on the preimage of the central charges for σα,β\sigma_{\alpha, \beta}. Take z=β+iαz = \beta + i\alpha: [ΦH]([(1z)])=[(a+bzc+dz)]=[(1c+dza+bz)]\begin{align*} \left[\Phi^H\right] \left( \left[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ z \end{pmatrix} \right] \right) &= \left[ \begin{pmatrix} a + b z \\ c + d z \end{pmatrix} \right] \\ & = \left[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \dfrac{c + d z}{a + b z} \end{pmatrix} \right] \end{align*} This therefore prooves the action of ΦH\Phi^H on the equivalence classes of stability conditions in the statement of the theorem.
